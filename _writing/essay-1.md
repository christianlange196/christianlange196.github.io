## Thesis
Optimization is valuable, but local maximization often ignores system-wide constraints and second-order effects.

## Argument and Analysis
In software and organizations, objective functions are usually incomplete. Over-optimizing visible metrics can degrade resilience, adaptability, and human judgment.

A practical approach is to optimize within guardrails: monitor tradeoffs, preserve slack, and include qualitative feedback loops.
